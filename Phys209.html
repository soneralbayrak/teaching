What is this course?

For a formal answer to this question, see

https://catalog.metu.edu.tr/course.php?course_code=2300209

More informally, this course introduces the differential equations and expands on how one can approach (and, if possible, solve) these objects. We are interested in these objects because most of the known physics is expressed in terms of differential equations.

Who can/should take this course?

This is a must course for any undergraduate student in the department of Physics, see the curriculum! Assuming that you have taken a sufficient set of prerequisite courses (see academic catalog), you should take this one too.

What are the course policies?

General:

  • You accept by taking this course that you will abide by the academic integrity rules within this course as well. You should definitely read my explanations of the expected academic integrity.
  • The medium of instruction in this course is English (article 5/1 in Regulations): you are of course free to use any language (Turkish, English, Quenya, etc.) when interacting with the teaching assistant or the instructor outside the classroom, but please do your best to stick to English inside the classroom!
  • Class attendance is mandatory by the Regulations (article 23/1). Although I am personally against mandatory attendance, I would strongly advice it anyway: Phys209 is a very essential course for the rest of your physics career and you may not be able to learn everything by studying yourself! That said, your attendance will not affect your grade.
  • The textbook for this class is “Elementary Differential Equations and Boundary Value Problems” by Boyce and Diprima (10th edition). You may also consult to “Advanced Calculus for Applications” (Hildebrand) and “Mathematical Methods in the Physical Sciences” (Boas) for supplementary reading.
  • There are some notes that I’ve been updating irregularly; you may check them here. For the source, see the files in github.

What will you learn in this course?

(1) Definition and Classification of Differential Equations: ordinary vs partial, linear vs non-linear, homogeneous vs non-homogeneous, 1st order vs 2nd order vs 3rd order vs … ⁡

(2) Linear equations with constant coefficients: Their relevance in physics and engineering, principle of superposition, characteristic equation, repeated roots, formal solution for both homogeneous and nonhomogeneous cases, initial and boundary value problems, linear independence and Wronskian, the Laplace transform, convolution

(3) Linear homogeneous equations with functional coefficients: Their relevance in physics and engineering, known soluble cases, reduction of order, series solutions (Frobenius Method), Sturm-Liouville theory and Fourier series

(4) Linear nonhomogeneous equations with functional coefficients: Method of undetermined coefficients, method of variation of parameters, conversion to systems of first order linear equations

(5) First order differential equations and their formal solutions: Linear equations, examples of soluble non-linear equations (separable, exact, Bernoulli), integrating factors

(6) Partial differential equations: Separation of variables, Laplace equation, physical examples
